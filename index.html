Who We Are

About Georgia Allied

The original incarnation of Georgia Allied Health Institute, Inc., was started sixteen years ago in 1990 when Ms. Rose Tabi Ndamukong was associated with a training program for hard-to-employ individuals at the New York Department of Social Services.

Inspired by her work with these programs, Ms. Tabi established a permanent job training and counseling center to provide hands-on job training for individuals striving for financial independence, a drug-free life, and a fulfilling career.

Georgia Allied Health Institute remains committed to empowering individuals by providing quality education, skill development, and a supportive community to foster professional growth.

Our Mission

At Georgia Allied Health Institute, our mission is to provide high-quality allied health education that empowers diverse students and communities, guiding them toward rewarding careers in the healthcare industry.

Our Objectives

  • Deliver Quality Education – Equip students with marketable skills for the healthcare industry.
  • Workforce Training – Provide hands-on occupational programs to enhance career readiness.
  • Professional Preparedness – Develop essential workplace skills for success.
  • Student Success Support – Offer academic resources and services to foster learning.
  • Industry Partnerships – Collaborate with businesses, government agencies, and institutions to expand career opportunities.
  • Community Impact – Improve lives through education and career advancement.
  • Future-Ready Approach – Continuously assess, prioritize, and enhance programs to meet evolving industry needs.

What We Do

What We Provide

  • Comprehensive counseling and support services to maximize student success.
  • Ongoing evaluation and updates to our hands-on training programs.
  • Modern training facilities and up-to-date equipment.
  • Highly qualified instructors with expertise in PCT (Patient Care Technician) and EKG/Phlebotomy.
  • Encouragement of self-discipline and motivation for success in careers and society.

Our Facilities

Our facility is specially designed for training students in the medical field. Located in Hazlehurst, GA in Gwinnett County, it provides ample training and classroom space, administrative offices, restrooms, and student lounge areas.

Our Classroom Equipment

We currently have “state-of-the-art” classroom and lab equipment to give students various means to study.

Our facilities include practice mannequins and audio/video equipment for educational and training use.

Applying For Admission

The admissions procedure is based on a free exchange of information between the applicant and the school. A personal interview is conducted before enrollment to discuss programs and career goals.

A School Director reviews each application to ensure high school graduation, a diploma, GED, or transcript from an accredited institution. If an applicant is not accepted for a program, an alternative may be suggested.

Admissions Requirements:

  • Visit the school
  • Complete an entrance interview with the admissions department
  • Fill out the admissions package
  • Make financial arrangements before classes start
  • Sign the enrollment agreement

Tuition & Fees

Registration Fees

A nonrefundable registration fee of $50 is charged to all students who apply for admission to enroll. Payment of this fee is valid for a period of one year from the date of initial application. Should an individual fail to enroll within this period, reapplication for admission and payment of the registration fee is required. The registration fee is assessed upon enrollment and is non-refundable unless the student is not accepted for enrollment or meets the conditions of cancellation as outlined in the refund policy.

Tuition and Fees

Georgia Allied Health Institute reserves the right to modify tuition and other charges upon sufficient notice to students. Announced tuition increases will not apply to students who maintain continuous attendance in a program of study.Tuition is based on scheduled hours of attendance.Tuition charges are assessed and payable as arranged when the student enrolls.The charges for each program are detailed in the tuition section of the student catalog and handbook.

Re-Start Fees

Students who wish to re-enter Georgia Allied Health Institute after having voluntarily or involuntarily withdrawn for any reason from their program of study may restart with Director Approval.
A $50 registration fee will be assessed.

Transcript Fees

First transcript is free. Additional copies available for $20 each.

Graduation Requirements

Georgia Allied Health Institute awards certificates for successful completion of full-time day and/or evening programs. Students must meet the certificate of completion requirements as outlined in the Academic Catalog current at the time of application for graduation.

Completion Requirements

  • Successfully complete all courses in the program of study.
  • Transfer of credit from another institution or proficiency credit may be used.
  • Certificates will not be awarded if the highest grade recorded is “F”, “W”, or “I”.
  • Earn a cumulative GPA of 2.0 (grade of “C” or better) for all coursework.
  • Must be in good financial standing with the school.

Certification Tests

Programs prepare students to take certification exams with respective organizations:

  • Phlebotomy Technician – NHA Certification
  • Patient Care Technician – NHA Certification
  • Medical Assistant – NHA Certification
  • EKG Technician – NHA Certification
  • Pharmacy Technician – NHA Certification
  • Diagnostic Medical Sonography – ARDMS Registry Exam
  • CNA – Georgia Nurses Assistant Licensing Exam
  • Dialysis Technician – CCHT Exam

Certification of Completion

Certificates of Completion are awarded upon satisfactory completion of all courses in the student’s program. A degree or diploma will not be issued nor will record of graduation of the program be posted to a student’s academic record until successful completion of the all requirements for graduation is met. If a student does not complete the necessary credit or courses for graduation, a certificate of completion indicating the amount of credit hours earned will be awarded.

Become a Certified Patient Care Technician

Prepare for a Rewarding Career in Healthcare with Expert Training in Patient Care, EKG, Phlebotomy, and More.

Patient Care Technician program

The Patient Care Technician program is designed to prepare graduates to assist patients with daily living needs, EKG monitoring, cardiac circulation, blood pressure monitoring, phlebotomy, and venipuncture skills.

Students will be trained in patient care techniques that help patients attain and maintain functional independence while respecting their rights. Graduates will also be prepared to take national certification exams for Patient Care Technician, Phlebotomy Technician, EKG Technician, and Clinical Medical Assisting.

Requirement: All prospective students must complete the TABE test and score 6.0 or higher.

Program Objectives

  • Perform basic first aid
  • Administer CPR/AED
  • Take vital signs accurately
  • Apply proper body mechanics in bed making, lifting, and turning patients
  • Demonstrate hand washing and cleanliness skills
  • Gain an introduction to all body systems
  • Perform electrocardiograms (EKG)
  • Master phlebotomy and venipuncture techniques

Medical Assistant Program

The Medical Assisting (MA) program prepares students for entry-level positions in medical facilities, including physicians’, chiropractors’, and podiatrists’ offices and clinics. The program combines theoretical instruction with limited hands-on training in essential clinical and office tasks.

Become a Certified Phlebotomy Technician

Develop essential skills in blood collection and laboratory procedures for a career in healthcare.

Phlebotomy Technician Program

The Phlebotomy Technician program equips students with the necessary skills to safely and efficiently collect and process blood specimens from patients of all ages. Training includes vacuum collection devices, syringes, capillary punctures, butterfly needles, and blood culture specimen collection.

Emphasis is placed on infection prevention, patient identification, proper specimen labeling, and quality assurance. The program also covers specimen handling, processing, and testing procedures such as manual hematocrits, urinalysis dipsticks, fecal occult blood tests, erythrocyte sedimentation rate, and pregnancy testing.

Requirement: Students must successfully complete the prerequisite course or obtain instructor approval.

Program Objectives

  • Master safe and effective blood collection techniques
  • Develop strong interpersonal communication skills
  • Gain an introduction to healthcare professions and clinical laboratory science
  • Understand the healthcare delivery system and medical terminology
  • Demonstrate infection control and safety procedures
  • Learn the anatomy and physiology of body systems
  • Associate lab departments with patient test evaluations
  • Perform venipuncture and capillary puncture techniques
  • Understand requisitioning, specimen transport, and processing

Program Details

Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR) Training

Learn life-saving skills to respond effectively in emergency situations.

Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR) Course

This CPR training course equips students with the essential skills to perform cardiopulmonary resuscitation on adults, children, and infants. The training covers one and two-rescuer scenarios, use of bag valve masks, airway obstruction management, Automated External Defibrillators (AED), and barrier devices.

The course also includes the Heartsaver First Aid module, which teaches choking relief techniques for adults and children, along with guidance on managing medical emergencies and injuries until professional help arrives.

Certification: Students who successfully complete the course and pass the written and skills exam will receive a course completion card, following American Heart Association (AHA) guidelines.

What You Will Learn

  • Perform CPR on adults, children, and infants
  • Learn one and two-rescuer CPR techniques
  • Use bag valve masks and barrier devices
  • Handle foreign body airway obstructions (choking)
  • Operate an Automated External Defibrillator (AED)
  • Provide first aid for medical emergencies and injuries
  • Understand emergency response procedures before professional help arrives

Program Details

Certification: American Heart Association (AHA)
Course Type: Hands-on & Theoretical
Completion: Written & Skills Exam
Issued Certificate: CPR Completion Card

Become a Certified Electrocardiogram Technician

Gain the essential skills needed to record and interpret EKG tracings, ensuring accurate cardiac assessments in healthcare settings.

Electrocardiogram Technician Program

The Electrocardiogram (EKG) Technician Program prepares students for careers in cardiac testing facilities, hospitals, and clinics. Students will be trained to record 3-lead, 5-lead, and 12-lead EKG reports while ensuring patient safety and infection control.

Graduates will be equipped with the skills to analyze EKG tracings, detect arrhythmias, and operate electrocardiogram equipment effectively.

Requirement: Students must complete prerequisite coursework or obtain instructor approval before enrolling.

Program Objectives

  • Perform accurate 3-lead, 5-lead, and 12-lead EKG recordings
  • Identify and remove artifacts in EKG tracings
  • Analyze and interpret EKG waveforms
  • Ensure proper lead placement and patient safety
  • Maintain and troubleshoot EKG machines
  • Recognize life-threatening arrhythmias
  • Measure heart rate from an EKG tracing
  • Follow standard infection prevention protocols

Program Details

Become a Certified Dialysis Technician

Gain the skills needed to assist in dialysis treatment, patient care, and essential medical procedures.

Dialysis Technician Program

The Dialysis Technician program is a comprehensive program designed to prepare students for careers as certified clinical hemodialysis technicians. The program combines theoretical instruction with hands-on training in patient care, venipuncture, and dialysis procedures.

Graduates will gain the necessary skills to monitor dialysis equipment, record vital signs, administer local anesthetics under supervision, and respond to patient emergencies.

Requirement: Students must complete any prerequisite courses or obtain instructor permission before entry.

Program Objectives

  • Prime dialysis equipment and monitor performance
  • Record patient weight and vital signs pre- and post-treatment
  • Administer local anesthetics and drugs under RN supervision
  • Monitor patients during dialysis for any complications
  • Take emergency measures, including CPR or oxygen administration

Student Grievance Procedure

Georgia Allied Health Institute provides a prompt and equitable process for resolving student grievances. The procedure is available to any student who believes that a school decision or action has adversely affected his/her status, rights, or privileges as a student.

Students with a grievance must first make a reasonable effort to resolve the issue on an informal basis with faculty or administrative personnel. If the issue is not resolved to the student’s satisfaction, the student may meet with a School Director who shall review the grievance with all parties concerned.

Externship and Job Placement Responsibilities

Guidelines for Externs and Students Seeking Job Placement Support

Responsibilities of Externs

  • Students are eligible for an externship after all classes they are attending have been completed. Students with a balance at GAHI will not be able to enroll in an externship.
  • If a student has their own externship site, they are to inform the Externship Coordinator. All sites must be approved by the Externship Coordinator.
  • Externs are to submit time sheets every week to the Externship Coordinator.
  • Externs are to be professional and treat the extern site as a job.
  • Externs must maintain regular communication with the externship coordinator by email or phone calls.
  • No student may withdraw themselves from an externship site until adequate safeguard measures have been taken by the Externship Coordinator.
  • Any student who fails to show up to an externship or is dropped from the externship site due to being unprofessional will be dropped from the program.

Responsibilities of Students Needing Help with Job Placement

  • Students are responsible for setting up an appointment with the Job Coordinator to receive counseling on how to build a correct resume.
  • The Job Placement Coordinator will provide students with counseling and assistance so that they feel ready to take interviews and secure a job.
  • Students who are hired are to report the name of the site to the Job Placement Coordinator.
